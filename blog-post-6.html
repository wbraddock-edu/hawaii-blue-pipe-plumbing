When to Call a Plumber vs. DIY: A Homeowner's Guide

April 28, 2024 · Blue Pipe Plumbing Hawaii

The internet has made DIY home repair more accessible than ever, and there are genuinely some plumbing tasks that competent homeowners can handle themselves. But there are also situations where DIY attempts make problems significantly worse, void insurance coverage, or create safety hazards. Knowing the difference is valuable knowledge for every homeowner.

Generally Safe to DIY

Replacing a faucet aerator, swapping out a showerhead, fixing a running toilet with a flapper valve replacement, unclogging a drain with a basic plunger, and replacing a supply line under a sink are all straightforward tasks that most homeowners can safely accomplish with basic tools and good video tutorials. These involve no structural changes, no gas lines, and no significant risk of flooding if done carefully.

Proceed with Caution — Know Your Limits

Replacing a toilet, installing a new faucet, or fixing a P-trap under a sink are tasks that are more complex but still manageable for handy homeowners. The key is knowing when to stop — if you open up the wall and find something unexpected, or if any step doesn't match the tutorial you're following, stop and call a professional rather than improvising.

Always Call a Plumber For...

Any work involving your main water line or main shutoff valve should be handled by a licensed plumber. Gas water heater installation or repair involves gas lines and requires a licensed contractor by Hawaii law. Any work that requires opening walls, floors, or slabs — including leak repair in inaccessible areas — needs professional equipment and expertise. Sewer line problems, whether backups or suspected damage, should always be diagnosed with professional camera equipment before any repair is attempted.

Here's the bottom line: the cost of a professional fix is almost always less than the cost of fixing a DIY repair that went wrong. When in doubt, a quick call to us costs nothing and helps you make an informed decision.
