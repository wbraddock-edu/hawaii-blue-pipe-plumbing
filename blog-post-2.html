Hurricane Season Plumbing Prep: A Kauai Guide

June 14, 2024 · Blue Pipe Plumbing Hawaii

Kauai is the most hurricane-exposed island in the Hawaiian chain. Hurricane Iniki's 1992 direct hit remains one of the most destructive storms in US history, and Kauai has faced near-misses and tropical storm impacts multiple times since. For plumbers, the aftermath of major storms reveals the same vulnerabilities every time — and most of them are preventable with proper preparation.

Before Hurricane Season (May–November)

Know where your main water shutoff valve is and make sure it operates freely. If you haven't turned it in years, it may be seized — call us to test and exercise it before you need it in an emergency. Have your water heater inspected — a damaged water heater after a storm can cause flooding and water damage that's worse than the storm itself.

What to Do When a Storm is Approaching

Fill your bathtubs with water for emergency use — a standard bathtub holds 80–100 gallons, which provides drinking water for days. Turn off your main water supply if you're evacuating or if the forecast is for a direct hit — this prevents flooding if pipes are damaged while you're away. Move outdoor hoses, sprinklers, and portable fixtures inside to prevent them becoming projectiles.

Post-Storm Inspection Before Turning Water Back On

Before restoring water service after a major storm, have a licensed plumber inspect your pipes if the property experienced structural damage. Broken pipes can cause catastrophic flooding when water pressure is restored. Check your water heater for physical damage before turning it back on. If your property is in a flood zone, do not use any electrical appliances or turn on the water heater until a licensed professional has cleared the electrical system.

We offer post-storm emergency inspections and prioritize existing customers during recovery periods. Having our number in your phone before a storm means faster service when you need it most.
