Water Conservation Tips for Kauai Homeowners

May 12, 2024 · Blue Pipe Plumbing Hawaii

There's a beautiful irony in Kauai's water situation: one side of the island receives over 400 inches of rain per year (among the highest anywhere on Earth), while the other side is technically a desert receiving under 20 inches. This variability, combined with the island's growing population and the pressures of climate change on precipitation patterns, means water conservation genuinely matters here — even in the rainiest state in the country.

Fix Leaks Promptly

A running toilet can waste 200 gallons per day — that's 6,000 gallons per month and about $50 in water costs. A dripping faucet wastes 3-20 gallons per day depending on the drip rate. A visibly leaking pipe is obvious, but even small leaks in supply lines behind walls can waste enormous amounts of water over time. Our electronic leak detection service can identify hidden leaks that would otherwise go unnoticed for years.

Upgrade to WaterSense Fixtures

EPA WaterSense certified toilets use 1.28 gallons per flush versus 3.5-7 gallons in older models. Low-flow showerheads reduce shower water use by 40% without a noticeable reduction in shower quality. WaterSense faucet aerators reduce faucet flow from 2.2 gpm to 1.5 gpm. The total water savings from upgrading a typical home's fixtures can exceed 30,000 gallons per year.

Rainwater Harvesting on Kauai

Hawaii law allows rainwater collection for outdoor use in most counties, and Kauai's rainfall makes this an excellent option for irrigation. A basic collection system — gutters, downspout diverter, and storage tank — can be installed for $500-1,500 and significantly reduces municipal water use for gardening and landscaping. We can design and install systems that integrate with your existing plumbing.

Water conservation also reduces your hot water use, which accounts for a significant portion of home energy costs. Every gallon of cold water you use instead of hot water saves energy in addition to water. Small behavioral changes — shorter showers, full loads of laundry, running the dishwasher only when full — add up to meaningful annual savings.
